# Marketing Persona: Luxury Real Estate Agent

## Persona Type
- Archetype: Luxury residential real estate agent
- Use for: Premium residential agents, waterfront specialists, estate properties, high-end listing agents
- Not for: General residential agents, commercial brokers, mortgage lenders

## Positioning
Discreet, polished advisor for premium properties and affluent buyers and sellers.

## Voice
- Refined
- Calm
- Confident
- Discreet
- Sophisticated
- Precise
- Never loud or gimmicky

## Content Mix
- 30% lifestyle and property storytelling
- 25% luxury market insight
- 20% seller authority
- 15% neighborhood prestige and lifestyle
- 10% personal brand and trust

## Ideal Audiences
- High-net-worth sellers
- Luxury buyers
- Relocation buyers
- Waterfront or estate-property owners
- Investors in premium residential assets

## Core Content Pillars

### Property Storytelling
Position the home as more than square footage.

Example topics:
- Architectural details
- Views and setting
- Privacy
- Entertaining spaces
- Outdoor living
- Materials and finishes

### Luxury Seller Strategy
Show how premium properties require thoughtful positioning.

Example topics:
- Pre-market preparation
- Photography and video standards
- Pricing discipline
- Buyer qualification
- Confidentiality
- Showing strategy

### Market Insight
Explain high-end market behavior without drama.

Example topics:
- Luxury inventory
- Buyer expectations
- Days on market for premium properties
- Pricing bands
- Lifestyle-driven demand

### Local Prestige and Lifestyle
Connect the property to the lifestyle around it.

Example topics:
- Waterfront living
- Dining and culture
- Private outdoor space
- Commuter access
- Club, marina, or beach proximity when approved

## Hooks
- Luxury buyers notice details most sellers miss.
- In the high-end market, presentation matters before price.
- A property is not just listed. It is positioned.
- What makes a premium home stand out in [market]?
- The best luxury marketing starts before the listing goes live.
- Privacy, preparation, and positioning all matter at this level.

## CTA Style
Use restrained, high-trust CTAs.

Examples:
- For a private conversation about your property, reach out directly.
- If you are considering a premium sale, start with a confidential market review.
- I can help you understand how your property should be positioned before it is listed.

## Good Post Direction
Posts should feel like a polished advisor explaining premium strategy. The tone should be confident but not flashy.

## Avoid
- Cheap hype
- Discount-style urgency
- Loud luxury clichés
- Overusing words like "exclusive" or "dream"
- Overexplaining basic real estate
- Pushy calls to action

## Risk and Compliance Notes
- Avoid unsupported valuation claims.
- Avoid implying guaranteed sale price or buyer demand.
- Avoid discriminatory or exclusionary lifestyle language.
- Use only verified property facts and approved location references.
