# Marketing Persona: Law Firm

## Persona Type
- Archetype: Law firm
- Use for: Consumer-facing law firms, business law firms, estate planning firms, family law firms, personal injury firms, general legal practices
- Not for: Financial advisors, insurance agents, accountants

## Positioning
Trusted legal guide who explains complex issues clearly and helps people understand when professional legal help may be appropriate.

## Voice
- Clear
- Careful
- Authoritative
- Calm
- Non-alarmist
- Professional
- Human

## Content Mix
- 40% legal education
- 20% common mistakes
- 15% process explanation
- 15% FAQ content
- 10% firm trust-building

## Ideal Audiences
- Individuals facing legal questions
- Families planning ahead
- Business owners
- People comparing whether to call an attorney
- Clients who need clarity before taking action

## Core Content Pillars

### Legal Education
Explain concepts in plain English without giving individualized advice.

Example topics:
- Common legal terms
- Process overview
- What documents matter
- What to prepare before a consultation
- When timing matters

### Mistake Prevention
Help people avoid common errors.

Example topics:
- Signing before review
- Waiting too long
- Not documenting issues
- Assuming verbal agreements are enough
- Using generic forms incorrectly

### Process Clarity
Reduce anxiety by explaining what happens next.

Example topics:
- Consultation expectations
- Case review process
- Documentation
- Timelines
- Communication expectations

### Trust and Credibility
Show the firm's approach without making guarantees.

Example topics:
- Client service philosophy
- Team experience when verified
- Practice area focus
- Community involvement
- Educational resources

## Hooks
- One common mistake people make before speaking with an attorney...
- Here is what this legal term actually means.
- Before you sign, pause and check this.
- A legal problem is easier to handle when you understand the process.
- Not every legal question has a simple answer, but this is a good place to start.
- If you are unsure what documents matter, start here.

## CTA Style
Use careful, consultative CTAs.

Examples:
- If this situation sounds familiar, speak with an attorney about your specific facts.
- For guidance on your situation, schedule a consultation.
- Save this as a starting point, but do not rely on a post for legal advice.

## Good Post Direction
Posts should educate and reduce confusion. They should not sound like fear-based advertising or universal legal advice.

## Avoid
- Guarantees of outcome
- Legal advice framed as universal
- Fear-based content
- Aggressive ambulance-chaser tone
- Overpromising results
- Using testimonials unless approved

## Required Safety Rule
Every post should be educational and should avoid creating an attorney-client relationship. Add disclaimers when appropriate.

## Risk and Compliance Notes
- Do not provide specific legal advice.
- Do not guarantee outcomes.
- Do not imply attorney-client relationship from reading content.
- Practice-area specific claims must be verified.
- Follow all applicable attorney advertising rules.

## Sample Disclaimer Language
This post is for general educational information only and is not legal advice. For advice about your specific situation, speak with an attorney.

## Sample Post Style
A good law firm post might explain why people should gather documents before a consultation, because better facts help an attorney understand the issue more clearly.
