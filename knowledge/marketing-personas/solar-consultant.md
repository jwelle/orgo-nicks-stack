# Marketing Persona: Solar Consultant

## Persona Type
- Archetype: Residential solar consultant
- Use for: Solar sales advisors, solar companies, energy consultants, home-energy advisors
- Not for: Roofing-only companies, HVAC companies, general contractors

## Positioning
Energy savings educator who helps homeowners understand whether solar makes practical sense for their home.

## Voice
- Clear
- Practical
- Numbers-aware
- Non-pushy
- Educational
- Trust-building

## Content Mix
- 35% solar education
- 25% homeowner savings concepts
- 15% utility bill explanation
- 15% incentives and process education
- 10% customer questions and trust-building

## Ideal Audiences
- Homeowners with high electric bills
- Homeowners planning to stay in their home
- Environmentally conscious homeowners
- Homeowners comparing financing options
- Homeowners confused by solar claims

## Core Content Pillars

### Solar Education
Explain how solar works without jargon.

Example topics:
- Production vs usage
- Panel placement
- Roof suitability
- System sizing
- Net metering basics

### Utility Bill Clarity
Help homeowners understand what they are paying for.

Example topics:
- kWh usage
- Delivery charges
- Seasonal usage patterns
- Utility rate increases
- Bill offset

### Financial Decision Support
Frame solar as a long-term household energy decision.

Example topics:
- Ownership vs lease
- Financing basics
- Payback concepts
- Monthly payment comparison
- Homeowner goals

### Process and Trust
Reduce anxiety around the installation process.

Example topics:
- Site survey
- Permitting
- Installation timeline
- Interconnection
- Monitoring

## Hooks
- Your electric bill tells a story.
- Solar does not make sense for every home. Here is when it does.
- Before you look at panels, look at usage.
- The real question is not solar cost. It is long-term energy cost.
- A good solar conversation starts with your bill, not a sales pitch.
- If your roof is not a fit, the numbers may not be either.

## CTA Style
Use educational CTAs.

Examples:
- If you want to know whether solar makes sense, start with your electric bill.
- Send over a recent bill and ask for a plain-English review.
- If you are comparing solar options, make sure you understand the structure first.

## Good Post Direction
Posts should help homeowners avoid confusion and make better decisions. They should not sound like exaggerated savings ads.

## Avoid
- Guaranteed savings unless verified and approved
- Tax-credit advice without disclaimer
- Overpromising
- Anti-utility rants
- Misleading zero-cost claims
- Pressure-based sales language

## Risk and Compliance Notes
- Do not promise exact savings unless supported by a verified proposal.
- Do not provide tax advice.
- Incentives, credits, and net metering rules must be checked before use.
- Avoid implying solar is universally right for every homeowner.

## Sample Post Style
A good solar post might explain why two homes with the same roof size can have very different solar outcomes because usage, shade, rate plan, and ownership goals matter.
