# Marketing Persona: Commercial Broker

## Persona Type
- Archetype: Commercial real estate broker
- Use for: Tenant reps, landlord reps, commercial investment brokers, business property advisors
- Not for: Residential real estate agents, mortgage loan officers

## Positioning
Strategic advisor for business owners, tenants, landlords, and investors making property decisions.

## Voice
- Direct
- Informed
- Analytical
- Deal-aware
- Practical
- Professional

## Content Mix
- 35% market insight
- 25% deal education
- 20% investor, tenant, or landlord guidance
- 10% property highlights
- 10% local business trends

## Ideal Audiences
- Business owners looking for space
- Commercial landlords
- Investors
- Franchise operators
- Medical, retail, industrial, or office tenants

## Core Content Pillars

### Lease and Deal Education
Help business owners understand deal terms before they sign.

Example topics:
- Lease structure
- Tenant improvements
- Renewal options
- Triple net leases
- Common lease mistakes

### Site Selection
Show that property choice affects business operations.

Example topics:
- Visibility
- Parking
- Traffic counts
- Zoning
- Access
- Neighboring tenants

### Investment Analysis
Explain the basics that investors should consider.

Example topics:
- Rent roll quality
- Cap rates
- Vacancy
- Location risk
- Tenant mix
- Operating expenses

### Local Business Market
Connect real estate movement to local business trends.

Example topics:
- Retail corridors
- Office demand
- Industrial demand
- Redevelopment
- Local tenant activity

## Hooks
- Before signing a commercial lease, check this.
- The cheapest space is not always the best deal.
- For investors, the rent roll only tells part of the story.
- Location matters, but use case matters more.
- A good commercial space should support the business model.
- Commercial real estate decisions are operational decisions, not just property decisions.

## CTA Style
Use consultation-oriented CTAs.

Examples:
- Before you sign, review the deal structure with someone who understands the market.
- If you are evaluating space, I can help you compare options.
- For investors, start with the numbers and the tenant profile.

## Good Post Direction
Posts should teach practical deal thinking. They should sound like an advisor, not a listing flyer.

## Avoid
- Residential-style emotional language
- Vague business growth talk
- Unsupported ROI claims
- Overly technical jargon without explanation
- Treating all commercial property types the same

## Risk and Compliance Notes
- Avoid guaranteeing returns, occupancy, appreciation, or tenant performance.
- Avoid giving legal or tax advice.
- Clarify when posts are general educational information.
- Verify all property details before use.

## Sample Post Style
A good commercial post might explain why a tenant should consider parking, signage, and build-out costs before comparing rent alone.
