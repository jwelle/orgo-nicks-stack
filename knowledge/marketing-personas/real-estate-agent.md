# Marketing Persona: Real Estate Agent

## Persona Type
- Archetype: Residential real estate agent
- Use for: General residential Realtors, local agents, buyer/seller agents, neighborhood-focused real estate professionals
- Not for: Luxury-only agents, commercial brokers, mortgage lenders

## Positioning
Local neighborhood advisor who helps buyers and sellers make confident real estate decisions.

## Voice
- Helpful
- Local
- Clear
- Friendly
- Practical
- Trustworthy
- Not overly salesy

## Content Mix
- 35% buyer and seller education
- 25% local community content
- 15% market updates
- 15% listings, wins, and success stories
- 10% personal trust-building

## Ideal Audiences
- First-time homebuyers
- Move-up families
- Downsizers
- Local homeowners considering selling
- Relocating buyers

## Core Content Pillars

### Buyer Education
Help buyers understand the process before they feel overwhelmed.

Example topics:
- Pre-approval basics
- Inspection expectations
- Offer strategy
- Common buyer mistakes
- What monthly payment really includes

### Seller Education
Help homeowners prepare before listing.

Example topics:
- Pricing strategy
- Simple prep before photos
- What buyers notice first
- Repairs worth considering
- How showings work

### Local Market Clarity
Explain what is happening in the local market without hype.

Example topics:
- Inventory shifts
- Days on market
- Buyer competition
- Pricing trends
- Seasonal patterns

### Community and Lifestyle
Show that the agent knows the area beyond listings.

Example topics:
- Neighborhood spotlights
- Local businesses
- Schools and parks
- Weekend events
- Town-specific moving considerations

## Hooks
- Thinking about buying in [town]? Start here.
- One thing sellers overlook before listing...
- Here's what today's buyers should know before making an offer.
- A quick [town] market note.
- Before you list your home, check this.
- The market is not one-size-fits-all. Here's what that means locally.

## CTA Style
Use soft, low-pressure CTAs.

Examples:
- If you are thinking about buying or selling, start with a quick conversation.
- Want a local market read for your neighborhood? Ask me.
- If you want to understand what your home may be worth, I can help you look at the numbers.
- Save this for later if a move is on your mind.

## Good Post Direction
Posts should sound like a knowledgeable local person explaining something useful. They should avoid sounding like a generic real estate ad.

## Avoid
- Overusing "dream home"
- Generic JUST LISTED posts with no context
- Fake urgency
- Empty market hype
- Posts that could apply to any agent in any town
- Pushy calls to action

## Risk and Compliance Notes
- Avoid claims about market performance unless supported by current data.
- Avoid discriminatory language or anything that could violate Fair Housing principles.
- Do not claim school quality, neighborhood safety, or demographic desirability unless the client has approved compliant language.
- Use verified listing facts only.

## Sample Post Style
A good seller prep post should teach one concrete thing, such as why the first photo matters, how clutter affects buyer perception, or why pricing strategy matters more than guessing high.
