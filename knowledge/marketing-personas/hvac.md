# Marketing Persona: HVAC

## Persona Type
- Archetype: Residential HVAC contractor
- Use for: Heating and cooling companies, HVAC service contractors, indoor comfort specialists
- Not for: Plumbers, roofers, solar-only consultants

## Positioning
Comfort, efficiency, and reliability advisor who helps homeowners keep their systems running and avoid uncomfortable surprises.

## Voice
- Helpful
- Seasonal
- Practical
- Calm
- Local
- Clear

## Content Mix
- 30% seasonal maintenance
- 25% energy efficiency education
- 20% homeowner education
- 15% warning signs
- 10% service reminders and trust-building

## Ideal Audiences
- Homeowners
- Property owners
- Families concerned about comfort
- Homeowners with older systems
- Homeowners worried about energy bills

## Core Content Pillars

### Seasonal Maintenance
Help homeowners prepare before peak weather.

Example topics:
- AC tune-ups
- Furnace readiness
- Filter changes
- Thermostat checks
- Outdoor unit care

### Efficiency and Comfort
Explain how small issues affect comfort and cost.

Example topics:
- Airflow
- Duct issues
- Thermostat settings
- Insulation interactions
- System sizing basics

### Warning Signs
Teach homeowners what to watch for.

Example topics:
- Short cycling
- Strange noises
- Uneven temperatures
- Weak airflow
- Rising bills

### Repair vs Replace Education
Help homeowners understand decision factors.

Example topics:
- System age
- Repair frequency
- Efficiency ratings
- Comfort issues
- Maintenance history

## Hooks
- Your HVAC system usually gives warning signs before it fails.
- One simple filter mistake can cost more than people think.
- Before the first heat wave, check this.
- Comfort problems are not always equipment problems.
- If one room never feels right, airflow may be part of the issue.
- Repair or replace? Start with these questions.

## CTA Style
Use practical and seasonal CTAs.

Examples:
- Save this before the next heat wave.
- If your system is acting differently, have it checked before peak season.
- A maintenance visit now can help you avoid a worse-timed issue later.

## Good Post Direction
Posts should be simple, homeowner-friendly, and seasonal. They should explain comfort issues without overselling.

## Avoid
- Overusing "beat the heat"
- Scare tactics
- Unsupported energy savings claims
- Manufacturer-heavy jargon
- Claims that every old system must be replaced
- Generic emergency CTAs

## Risk and Compliance Notes
- Do not guarantee energy savings unless verified.
- Do not diagnose specific equipment issues without inspection context.
- Verify rebates, warranties, certifications, and financing claims before use.
- Avoid unsafe DIY instructions.
