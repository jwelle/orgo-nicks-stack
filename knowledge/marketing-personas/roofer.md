# Marketing Persona: Roofer

## Persona Type
- Archetype: Roofing contractor
- Use for: Residential roofers, storm restoration roofers, roof replacement companies, roof repair companies
- Not for: Solar-only consultants, HVAC companies, plumbers

## Positioning
Local home-protection expert who helps homeowners spot roofing issues early and make informed repair or replacement decisions.

## Voice
- Practical
- Trustworthy
- Direct
- Seasonal
- Local
- Calm

## Content Mix
- 35% homeowner education
- 25% storm and seasonal readiness
- 15% warning signs
- 15% project and process explanation
- 10% local trust and proof

## Ideal Audiences
- Homeowners with older roofs
- Homeowners after storms
- Sellers preparing a home
- New homeowners
- Property owners worried about leaks

## Core Content Pillars

### Warning Signs
Teach homeowners what to look for before small issues become larger problems.

Example topics:
- Missing shingles
- Ceiling stains
- Granule loss
- Flashing problems
- Soft spots
- Gutter issues

### Seasonal Readiness
Prepare homeowners for weather-driven roofing risks.

Example topics:
- Spring inspections
- Summer storms
- Hurricane season where applicable
- Fall maintenance
- Winter ice and snow considerations

### Repair vs Replacement Education
Help homeowners understand options without pressure.

Example topics:
- When repair may be enough
- When replacement may be worth considering
- Roof age
- Material differences
- Inspection process

### Trust and Process
Explain how a good contractor works.

Example topics:
- What happens during an inspection
- Photo documentation
- Cleanup expectations
- Timeline
- Warranty basics

## Hooks
- A small roof issue rarely stays small.
- After heavy rain, check these three places.
- Not every roof leak starts where you think.
- Before replacing your roof, ask this question.
- Your roof usually gives warning signs before it becomes urgent.
- If your shingles look like this, it may be time for an inspection.

## CTA Style
Use practical, inspection-oriented CTAs.

Examples:
- If you are unsure what you are seeing, have it checked before it spreads.
- Save this list for after the next heavy storm.
- If your roof is older, a quick inspection can help you plan ahead.

## Good Post Direction
Posts should be useful, calm, and specific. They should help homeowners understand risk without panic.

## Avoid
- Fear-based panic
- Insurance guarantees
- Generic quality claims
- "We've got you covered"
- Storm-chaser language
- Unsupported claims about insurance approval

## Risk and Compliance Notes
- Do not guarantee insurance outcomes.
- Do not claim a roof needs replacement without inspection context.
- Verify licensing, warranty, and certification claims before use.
- Avoid implying urgency unless facts support it.

A good roofing post might explain why a ceiling stain can come from flashing, vents, or roof penetrations, not always the area directly above the stain.
