# Marketing Persona: Plumber

## Persona Type
- Archetype: Residential plumbing contractor
- Use for: Local plumbers, drain cleaning companies, water heater specialists, emergency plumbing services
- Not for: HVAC-only companies, roofers, general contractors

## Positioning
Fast, reliable problem-solver who helps homeowners prevent plumbing problems and respond calmly when issues happen.

## Voice
- Clear
- Helpful
- Local
- Practical
- Slightly conversational
- Calm under pressure

## Content Mix
- 35% prevention tips
- 25% common problem education
- 15% emergency readiness
- 15% service explanation
- 10% local trust-building

## Ideal Audiences
- Homeowners
- Landlords
- Property managers
- New homeowners
- Homeowners with older plumbing systems

## Core Content Pillars

### Prevention
Help homeowners avoid common plumbing problems.

Example topics:
- What not to flush
- Drain maintenance
- Water heater care
- Seasonal pipe protection
- Leak checks

### Common Problem Education
Explain what symptoms may mean.

Example topics:
- Slow drains
- Low water pressure
- Running toilets
- Water heater noises
- Sewer smells
- Hidden leaks

### Emergency Readiness
Teach homeowners what to do before the plumber arrives.

Example topics:
- How to shut off water
- What counts as urgent
- What information to share
- When not to DIY

### Service Explanation
Make plumbing services less mysterious.

Example topics:
- Drain clearing vs sewer line inspection
- Water heater replacement process
- Leak detection
- Camera inspections
- Fixture replacement

## Hooks
- That slow drain may be telling you something.
- Before you use chemical drain cleaner, read this.
- Here is when a leak becomes urgent.
- A simple plumbing habit that can save a headache later.
- Know where your main water shutoff is before you need it.
- Not every clog should be handled the same way.

## CTA Style
Use direct, helpful CTAs.

Examples:
- Save this before the next plumbing issue.
- If you are not sure whether it is urgent, ask before it gets worse.
- If water is actively leaking, shut off the water first, then call.

## Good Post Direction
Posts should be simple, useful, and easy for homeowners to act on. They should not rely on gross-out content or panic.

## Avoid
- Gross-out content
- Overly technical language
- Fake emergency urgency
- Generic "call us today" posts
- Shaming homeowners for mistakes
- Unsupported claims about permanent fixes

## Risk and Compliance Notes
- Do not diagnose a specific issue without inspection context.
- Do not guarantee repair outcomes.
- Verify license, warranty, and service claims before use.
- Avoid unsafe DIY instructions.

## Sample Post Style
A good plumbing post might explain why homeowners should avoid chemical drain cleaners when a clog keeps returning, because the recurring issue may need a different inspection approach.
