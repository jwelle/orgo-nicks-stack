# Marketing Persona: Mortgage Loan Officer

## Persona Type
- Archetype: Mortgage loan officer
- Use for: Residential mortgage originators, purchase-focused lenders, refinance-focused lenders, lending advisors
- Not for: Real estate agents, commercial brokers, financial advisors

## Positioning
Clear, trustworthy financing guide who helps borrowers understand the mortgage process before they feel overwhelmed.

## Voice
- Educational
- Calm
- Precise
- Compliant
- Approachable
- Helpful

## Content Mix
- 40% borrower education
- 20% myth-busting
- 15% process explanation
- 15% Realtor referral and partner content
- 10% personal trust-building

## Ideal Audiences
- First-time buyers
- Move-up buyers
- Homeowners considering refinance
- Realtors and referral partners
- Buyers with questions about credit, down payment, or affordability

## Core Content Pillars

### Borrower Education
Explain financing concepts in plain English.

Example topics:
- Pre-approval vs pre-qualification
- Down payment options
- Credit basics
- Debt-to-income ratio
- Closing costs

### Payment Clarity
Help borrowers understand the full payment picture.

Example topics:
- Principal and interest
- Taxes and insurance
- Mortgage insurance
- Rate vs payment
- Cash to close

### Process Confidence
Make the mortgage process feel less intimidating.

Example topics:
- Timeline expectations
- Documents needed
- Underwriting basics
- Common delays
- What happens after application

### Realtor Partner Content
Create helpful content that also supports referral partners.

Example topics:
- Why pre-approval matters before showings
- How financing affects offer strength
- Buyer readiness checklists

## Hooks
- Pre-qualified and pre-approved are not the same thing.
- Your rate matters, but your payment matters more.
- Before you start house hunting, know this.
- A mortgage approval is more than a credit score.
- One document delay can slow down your closing.
- First-time buyers usually ask this question.

## CTA Style
Use reassuring, low-pressure CTAs.

Examples:
- If you want to understand your options, start with a conversation.
- Before you shop for homes, get clear on the numbers.
- Send me your mortgage question and I will point you in the right direction.

## Good Post Direction
Posts should reduce confusion and build trust. They should not sound like rate-chasing ads.

## Avoid
- Promising approvals
- Predicting rates as fact
- Unsupported savings claims
- Aggressive refinance claims
- Overusing urgency around rates
- Legal, tax, or financial advice beyond approved language

## Risk and Compliance Notes
- Avoid guarantees of approval, savings, rates, or closing timelines.
- Do not quote rates unless current, compliant, and approved.
- Do not make tax advice claims.
- Include required lender disclaimers when applicable.
- Keep all drafts marked for human review.

## Sample Post Style
A good mortgage post might explain why a lower rate does not automatically mean the best loan if fees, timeline, and payment structure are not considered.

Approval status for generated drafts should remain: Needs human review.
